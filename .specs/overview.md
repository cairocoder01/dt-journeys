# Introduction

Disciple Tools has been built on a powerful and flexible database for managing contacts, groups, and any other type of data. However, the power of that database can be overwhelming to navigate and cause a loss of direction when viewing or editing the system. 

When we look at the interactions we have with individuals and groups through our ministries, what we really want to track and visualize are the journeys that they are on. Each of us have gone through different journeys to get to the place and be the person we are today. 

The Journeys feature is a new way to interact with Disciple Tools. It moves the powerful data into the background and helps us to focus on the journeys that we are leading our contacts and groups through as they seek out and grow in their walk with Jesus. 

Some of those journeys are already tracked in D.T. \- things like a general growth of faith /discipleship (faith milestones) or media follow-up process (seeker path and quick actions). Other journeys can be facilitated more easily going forward \- such as going through various Bible study story sets or following specific ministry models like T4T, Zume, or DMM.

All of us have been on different journeys in our life of faith. And in DT, contacts and groups could be going through multiple journeys at one time. Sometimes, when one journey ends, it leads to a new one. 

With the Disciple Tools Journey feature, you can get back to focusing on the journeys God is leading people on and you are disciplining them through and less on the data your leaders want to track.

By designing Journeys specific to your method of ministry, multipliers can easily know the next step to pursue and leadership can provide resources specific to reach of those steps, so disciples are always equipped and prepared for the task before them.

# User Interface

### Specifications

* Allow multiple active journeys per contact/group/record  
  * When viewing a contact’s journeys, it should also show journeys for the groups they are part of  
* Allow sequential and non-sequential journeys  
  * Sequential journeys   
    * Show as timeline  
    * Allow skipping steps  
    * Don’t force sequential completion, but encourage it  
  * Non-sequential journeys  
    * Show as either list or icon/grid  
    * Can be expanded to show full stage display (links, instructions, fields, etc)  
* Journey Stage display  
  * Action buttons to complete, skip, or mark as stalled/incomplete  
  * Display name, short description, and links  
    * Also completion date if completed  
  * Longer instructions available in pop-out interface  
    * These could include the full course material for study guides  
    * Rich text with formatting  
  * Additional linked fields  
    * Based on journey stage configuration, show fields that are relevant to be edited at this stage  
* Journey completion  
  * When all stages are complete, mark journey as complete  
  * At bottom/end of journey UI, show button to mark the journey as completed  
    * For sequential journeys, this would mark the remaining stages as skipped  
  * Upon completion of a journey, prompt to find another to start  
* Journey List/Dashboard  
  * List of all records that have a journey connected to them  
  * Include all record types together: contact, group, etc.  
  * Table/List with record name, status, and visual progress indicator of each active journey  
    * Hide completed journeys  
  * Basic filters  
    * All Viewable to user  
    * Assigned to user  
    * Favorite  
    * By Record Type (multi-select)  
      * Contact  
      * Group  
      * Etc.  
    * By Journey (multi-select
